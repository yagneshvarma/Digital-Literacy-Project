Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud (The “Refund” Scam)
What it is: UPI payment fraud is a cybercrime where scammers trick people into sending money by pretending to offer refunds, cashbacks, or prizes. They misuse UPI apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm to fool victims into authorizing payments.
How it happens (Step-by-Step):
Initial Contact: The scammer calls or messages the victim, pretending to be from a bank, customer care, or shopping website, saying a refund is pending.
Building Trust: They speak politely and may use basic details like your name or recent activity to appear genuine.
The Hook: The scammer sends a payment request or QR code, claiming it is needed to “receive” money.
The Trap: The victim is told to open the UPI app and enter their UPI PIN to process the refund.
The Theft: As soon as the PIN is entered, money is deducted from the victim’s account and transferred to the scammer.
Who is targeted: College students, online shoppers, and people using digital payments regularly are common targets. Scammers take advantage of their trust and lack of awareness about UPI rules.
Consequences: Victims face instant financial loss, which is often difficult to recover. It also causes stress, fear, and loss of trust in online payment systems
