PART B: Stay Safe Online: Cybersecurity Checklist for College Students
General Digital Safety:
Use Strong Passwords: Don’t keep simple passwords like your name or birthday. Try to use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Turn on 2FA for your email, social media, and banking apps. It adds an extra layer of safety.
Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Important Work: Don’t log into bank accounts or college portals using free Wi-Fi in cafes or campus.
Think Before Clicking Links: If you get random links about jobs, prizes, or urgent updates, don’t click them without checking.
Protect Your Personal Info: Avoid sharing your phone number, ID cards, or live location on social media.
Download Only from Trusted Sources: Install apps only from official stores. Don’t use cracked or unknown apps.
UPI & Financial Safety:
UPI PIN Rule: Remember, UPI PIN is only used to send money, not to receive money.
Avoid Unknown Payment Requests: Don’t accept requests from strangers in apps like Google Pay or PhonePe.
Double-Check Before Paying: Always check the receiver’s name before sending money. One small mistake can cost you.
How to Report Cybercrime in India:
Helpline: Call 1930 immediately if money is involved
Website: Report at https://cybercrime.gov.in
