Social Media Checklist:
The Do’s (Smart Moves):
Tighten your circle:
Go into your settings and make sure your private life stays private. Only let people you actually know follow your personal accounts.
Build a digital portfolio:
Post about your wins—like a project you finished or a certificate you earned. It makes a great impression.
Self-Audit:
Every few months, look up your own name on a search engine. If something looks bad, take it down.
The "Wait" Rule:
If a post feels risky or you're writing it while you're mad, save it as a draft. Come back 10 minutes later and you’ll probably delete it.
Connect with pros:
Use sites like LinkedIn to follow people who have the job you want one day.
The Dont's (Big Mistakes):
Keep the drama offline:
Don't use your stories or feed to complain about your school, your boss, or that one annoying professor. It always gets back to them.
Hide your details:
Don't post photos of your ID, your dorm number, or your daily routine. It’s a huge safety risk.
Stay out of the comments:
Don't get sucked into toxic arguments online. It’s a waste of energy and looks bad to recruiters.
Clean up the photos:
Avoid posting anything that looks like party central. Keep the blurry, late-night photos in your private camera roll.
Don't trust the "Delete" button:
Just because you deleted a post doesn't mean it's gone. Assume someone has a screenshot of everything you do online
